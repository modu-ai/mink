# Code of Conduct

GOOSE is built by and for people who want a calm, generous, lifelong companion to exist. The community we build around the project should reflect those same values.

This document records the norms we hold ourselves to. It is short by design — we expect contributors to read it once, and then to act with care.

---

## 1. Our Pledge

We pledge to make participation in AI.GOOSE and its community a welcoming experience for everyone, regardless of background, identity, or experience level.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, kind, and healthy community.

---

## 2. Our Standards

We hold ourselves to constructive standards. Examples we aim for:

- **Empathy first** — assume good intent; ask before assuming the worst
- **Specific, kind feedback** — critique the idea or the code, never the person
- **Generosity with credit** — name the contributors whose work you build on
- **Patience with newcomers** — everyone was new once; the answer to a beginner question is a teaching moment, not a gatekeeper test
- **Respect for time** — concise messages, clear scopes, focused PRs
- **Cultural awareness** — GOOSE is a global project; English is the lingua franca, and translations of UX strings are welcome and credited
- **Disagree productively** — surface tradeoffs, propose alternatives, accept the maintainer's final call when consensus cannot be reached

When something falls short of these standards, we address it directly, repair the harm, and move forward.

---

## 3. Maintainer Responsibilities

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these standards. They will take fair and proportionate corrective action in response to any contributor behavior they deem inappropriate, threatening, or out of step with this Code.

Maintainers may edit, remove, or decline contributions (commits, code, comments, edits, issues, and other artifacts) that are not aligned with this Code, and will communicate the reason when doing so.

---

## 4. Scope

This Code applies within all project spaces — the GitHub repository, Discussions, Issues, Pull Requests, Discord (when established), and any official off-platform communication channel — as well as when an individual is officially representing the project in public spaces.

---

## 5. Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that does not align with this Code, please report it privately:

- **Email**: `conduct@mo.ai.kr`
- **GitHub Private Advisory**: [Open a private report](https://github.com/modu-ai/goose/security/advisories/new) (you can use this channel for conduct issues, not only security)

All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. The maintainer team is committed to maintaining the privacy and safety of the reporter.

We will acknowledge receipt within 48 hours, complete a triage within 7 days, and follow up with the reporter at every step.

---

## 6. Resolution

When a Code violation is confirmed, the maintainer team will choose a proportionate response — typically one of:

1. **Private clarification** — a direct message explaining how the behavior fell short and what is expected going forward
2. **Public correction** — a brief public note when the behavior occurred in a public space and clarification serves the broader community
3. **Temporary cooling-off** — a short pause from project spaces (typically 1–7 days)
4. **Extended pause** — a longer separation when patterns repeat or escalate
5. **Permanent removal** — when behavior is severe or repeated despite prior steps

Decisions are documented internally with rationale and timeline. Reporters are informed of the resolution unless they request otherwise.

---

## 7. Appeals

Anyone affected by a maintainer decision may request a review by emailing `conduct@mo.ai.kr` within 14 days. A second maintainer not involved in the original decision will conduct the review.
